**Armed Forces Day**, known also as the **Feast of the Polish Armed Forces** ([Polish](/wiki/Polish_language "Polish language")
: _ÅšwiÄ™to Wojska Polskiego_), is a national holiday celebrated annually on 15 August in Poland, commemorating the anniversary of the 1920 victory over [Soviet Russia](/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic "Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic")
 at the [Battle of Warsaw](/wiki/Battle_of_Warsaw_(1920) "Battle of Warsaw (1920)")
 during the [Polishâ€“Soviet War](/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Soviet_War "Polishâ€“Soviet War")
.[\[1\]](#cite_note-PolskieRadio2013-1)
 Armed Forces Day is held in conjunction with the [Day of the Assumption of the Virgin Mary](/wiki/Assumption_of_Mary "Assumption of Mary")
, itself a separate public holiday.[\[2\]](#cite_note-Kontakt24-2)
 The event is marked by [military parades](/wiki/Military_parade "Military parade")
, equipment reviews, showcases and remembrances by all branches of the [Polish Armed Forces](/wiki/Polish_Armed_Forces "Polish Armed Forces")
 across the country. To commemorate the republic's victory over the Red Army, Minister of Military Affairs [StanisÅ‚aw Szeptycki](/wiki/Stanis%C5%82aw_Szeptycki "StanisÅ‚aw Szeptycki")
 established the Feast of the Soldier (_ÅšwiÄ™ta Å»oÅ‚nierza_), or Soldiers Day, in 1923.[\[4\]](#cite_note-4)
 In proclaiming the holiday, Szeptycki declared that, "\[o\]n the anniversary of the memorable defeat of the Bolshevik onslaught on Warsaw, we honor the memory of those killed in battles with enemies throughout all ages and for Polish independence."[\[5\]](#cite_note-Museum-5)
 The date of 15 August was chosen as it coincided with the [21st Mountain Division's](/wiki/21st_Mountain_Infantry_Division_(Poland) "21st Mountain Infantry Division (Poland)")
 evening counterattack on Russian troops, eventually forcing the Red Army's general retreat.[\[2\]](#cite_note-Kontakt24-2)
 Additionally, the government of the Second Republic desired the military's greater integration with the new state, as well as for the armed forces to improve its image among the civilian public.[\[2\]](#cite_note-Kontakt24-2)

The Feast of the Soldier remained a celebrated holiday until 1947, when the newly installed communist government of the [People's Republic of Poland](/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Poland "People's Republic of Poland")
 discontinued the tradition.[\[6\]](#cite_note-Gazeta2013-6)
 In 1950, the [Council of Ministers](/wiki/Cabinet_of_Poland "Cabinet of Poland")
 under Prime Minister [JÃ³zef Cyrankiewicz](/wiki/J%C3%B3zef_Cyrankiewicz "JÃ³zef Cyrankiewicz")
 passed regulations to officially celebrate the military on 12 October.[\[7\]](#cite_note-DzU1950-7)
 Renaming the celebration as Polish Armed Forces Day (_DzieÅ„ Wojska Polskiego_), the day marked the anniversary of the Soviet-organized [Polish 1st Tadeusz KoÅ›ciuszko Infantry Division](/wiki/Polish_1st_Tadeusz_Ko%C5%9Bciuszko_Infantry_Division "Polish 1st Tadeusz KoÅ›ciuszko Infantry Division")
 engaging [German](/wiki/Nazi_Germany "Nazi Germany")
 units at the [Battle of Lenino](/wiki/Battle_of_Lenino "Battle of Lenino")
 in October 1943.[\[7\]](#cite_note-DzU1950-7)
 The communist government found it impossible to celebrate the original date of 15 August and its coinciding historical significance, owing to its glorification of the [Red Army](/wiki/Red_Army "Red Army")
's defeat.[\[8\]](#cite_note-PolskieRadio2012-8)
 Following the return of democracy in the wake of the [events of 1989](/wiki/Revolutions_of_1989 "Revolutions of 1989")
, the [Sejm](/wiki/Sejm "Sejm")
 approved the restoration of Armed Forces Day to the original 15 August date and meaning on 30 July 1992, repealing the earlier communist era 1950 declaration.[\[9\]](#cite_note-Sejm1992-9)

Commemorations
--------------

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day_(Poland)&action=edit&section=2 "Edit section: Commemorations")\
\]

Armed Forces Day is celebrated throughout Poland, with military parades, commemorations for active personnel, veterans, and the dead. As head of state and [commander-in-chief](/wiki/Commander-in-chief "Commander-in-chief")
, the [President of Poland](/wiki/President_of_Poland "President of Poland")
 presides over promotions of high-ranking officers, including the promotion of colonels to generals at the [Presidential Palace](/wiki/Presidential_Palace,_Warsaw "Presidential Palace, Warsaw")
.[\[1\]](#cite_note-PolskieRadio2013-1)
 The pageantry continues with commemorations near the [Tomb of the Unknown Soldier](/wiki/Tomb_of_the_Unknown_Soldier,_Warsaw "Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, Warsaw")
 held in [PiÅ‚sudski Square](/wiki/Pi%C5%82sudski_Square "PiÅ‚sudski Square")
.[\[11\]](#cite_note-Prezydent-11)
 The events in Warsaw typically involve the participation of the nation's political establishment, including the [president](/wiki/President_of_Poland "President of Poland")
, the [prime minister](/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Poland "Prime Minister of Poland")
, and leading members of the [cabinet](/wiki/Cabinet_of_Poland "Cabinet of Poland")
.[\[6\]](#cite_note-Gazeta2013-6)
 Leading politicians also lay wreaths at the [JÃ³zef PiÅ‚sudski](/wiki/J%C3%B3zef_Pi%C5%82sudski "JÃ³zef PiÅ‚sudski")
 Monument, as well as at the [Tomb of the Unknown Soldier](/wiki/Tomb_of_the_Unknown_Soldier,_Warsaw "Tomb of the Unknown Soldier, Warsaw")
.[\[6\]](#cite_note-Gazeta2013-6)

As a [NATO](/wiki/NATO "NATO")
 member state, Armed Forces Day in Poland typically draws representatives from other allied militaries in attendance.[\[12\]](#cite_note-12)

The centennial celebrations for 2020 were "1920 Battle of Warsaw â€“ a Polish victory for the freedom of Europe".[\[13\]](#cite_note-13)

### Armed Forces Day Parade

\[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Armed_Forces_Day_(Poland)&action=edit&section=3 "Edit section: Armed Forces Day Parade")\
\]

One of the main attractions of the Warsaw festivities is a [military parade](/wiki/Military_parade "Military parade")
 of servicemen and women, as well as a review of existing military equipment, held normally along [UjazdÃ³w Avenue](/wiki/Ujazd%C3%B3w_Avenue "UjazdÃ³w Avenue")
.[\[11\]](#cite_note-Prezydent-11)
 Active servicemen and women participate at many events, joined often by volunteers appearing in historical dress, including those donning the equipment and dress of the [Polishâ€“Soviet War](/wiki/Polish%E2%80%93Soviet_War "Polishâ€“Soviet War")
 or other historical periods.[\[1\]](#cite_note-PolskieRadio2013-1)
 The first grand military parades since the holiday was reinstated in Warsaw were in 2007 and 2008, and parades have been held yearly since 2013. In 2019, the parade took place in the southern city of [Katowice](/wiki/Katowice "Katowice")
 to honour the participation of [Silesia](/wiki/Silesia "Silesia")
's residents in the [Silesian Uprisings](/wiki/Silesian_Uprisings "Silesian Uprisings")
 from 1919 to 1921.[\[14\]](#cite_note-14)

On 6 August 2024, during the [Russian invasion of Ukraine](/wiki/Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "Russian invasion of Ukraine")
,[\[36\]](#cite_note-dw.com-38)
[\[37\]](#cite_note-ft.com-39)

, the [Armed Forces of Ukraine](/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_Ukraine "Armed Forces of Ukraine")
 launched an incursion into [Russia](/wiki/Russia "Russia")
's [Kursk Oblast](/wiki/Kursk_Oblast "Kursk Oblast")
 and clashed with the [Russian Armed Forces](/wiki/Russian_Armed_Forces "Russian Armed Forces")
 and [Russian border guard](/wiki/FSB_Border_Service_of_Russia "FSB Border Service of Russia")
.[\[38\]](#cite_note-REU86-40)
[\[36\]](#cite_note-dw.com-38)
[\[37\]](#cite_note-ft.com-39)
 A [state of emergency](/wiki/State_of_emergency_in_Russia "State of emergency in Russia")
 was declared in Kursk Oblast,[\[39\]](#cite_note-BBC_8_Aug-41)
 and Russian reserves were rushed to the area.[\[40\]](#cite_note-BBC_Cursino-42)
 By the end of the first week, the Ukrainian military said it had captured 1,000Â km2 (390Â sqÂ mi) of Russian territory, while Russian authorities acknowledged that Ukraine had captured 28 settlements.[\[41\]](#cite_note-43)

In the second half of August the front stabilized,[\[42\]](#cite_note-forbes_20240911-44)
 and in early October, the Ukrainian advance had stalled.[\[11\]](#cite_note-CNN_Third_Month-12)
 From November, North Korean forces were sent to the province to support the Russian military. According to Russia, the incursion involved around 300 Ukrainian troops, 11 tanks, and over 20 [armored combat vehicles](/wiki/Armoured_fighting_vehicle "Armoured fighting vehicle")
,[\[67\]](#cite_note-MT86-69)
 and was aimed in two directions: at [Oleshnya](/wiki/Oleshnya,_Kursk_Oblast "Oleshnya, Kursk Oblast")

The attack began at 08:00 [MSK](/wiki/Moscow_Time "Moscow Time")

 Russian [milbloggers](/wiki/Milblog "Milblog")
 also largely dismissed the supposed raids as "unsuccessful" and a "media stunt".[\[68\]](#cite_note-ISW_August_6-70)

According to Russia, at least 1,000 troops crossed the border on the first day, supported by [tanks](/wiki/Tank "Tank")
 and armored vehicles.[\[39\]](#cite_note-BBC_8_Aug-41)
 According to Andrzej Wilk of the [Centre for Eastern Studies](/wiki/Centre_for_Eastern_Studies "Centre for Eastern Studies")

 Brigades, supported by artillery and air defense sub-units.[\[66\]](#cite_note-wilk-68)
 It was later claimed by members of the Ukrainian military that the breakthrough on the border was conducted by the 80th and 82nd Air Assault Brigades.[\[64\]](#cite_note-Kyrylenko-66)

Russian milbloggers claimed that the offensive was being performed by the [Russian Volunteer Corps](/wiki/Russian_Volunteer_Corps "Russian Volunteer Corps")
 (RVC), while the _[New Voice of Ukraine](/wiki/New_Voice_of_Ukraine "New Voice of Ukraine")
_ citing a source in [Ukraine's Main Military Intelligence](/wiki/Ukraine%27s_Main_Military_Intelligence "Ukraine's Main Military Intelligence")
 reported that the RVC was not present.[\[68\]](#cite_note-ISW_August_6-70)
